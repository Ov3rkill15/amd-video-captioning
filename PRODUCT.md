# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The developer recording the hackathon demo video, and the AMD ACT II judges watching that recording. Context: a screen capture, often compressed, viewed on a laptop. The UI must read crisply on video at a glance.

## Product Purpose

Local demo front-end for the Track 2 video-captioning agent. Feed it a video (local path or URL), watch the agent sample frames and generate four styled captions live. Success: a judge instantly understands what the pipeline does and trusts that it works.

## Brand Personality

Technical, confident, precise. A serious developer tool, not a toy: the captions provide the humor, the interface provides the credibility.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS landing gradients; playful pastel AI-tool aesthetics; anything that looks like a template. No cream/beige body backgrounds.

## Design Principles

1. The output is the hero: captions and sampled frames get the visual weight, chrome stays quiet.
2. Show the machinery: expose frames, timing, and model name because engineering transparency is the pitch.
3. Read well on video: high contrast, generous type, no hairline details that smear when compressed.
4. One accent, spent on action and state, never decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, visible focus states, reduced-motion alternative for the loading shimmer, keyboard-operable controls.
